Understanding Racism: Films Studies

Understanding the privilege, that we as white people hold, is crucial in helping our black brothers and sisters in the fight against systematic racism. I am used to standing tall and proud, flying the rainbow flag in the fight for same-sex marriage and often getting myself into arguments over homophobia at every family gathering. Yet, somehow this fight feels different to me. In a way, I want to say that I understand what my black friends face in their everyday lives fighting the system that has been fundamentally created to function agaisnt them, yet, I simply could never understand what that feels like. I will still protest, I will still get to the streets and fight hand-in-hand with my friends, yet this time around I don’t intend to lead, I intend to listen and follow.

In the meantime, I have compiled a list of films that have taught me about systematic racism and everyday lives of minorities around the world. Cinema is a great teacher, that has taught me many lessons and will continue to do so for many years to come. By sharing these films, I hope to amplify the voices of black protagonists, echo their plight and fill in the gap of understanding that at this time of need we should all stand behind our black brothers and sisters in the fight against systematic racism.

Precious 2009

City of God 2003

Moonlight 2016

La Haine 1995

Do the Right Thing 1989

Paris is Burning 1990

By, Jonas Leškevičius.
