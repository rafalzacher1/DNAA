About Us

Our manifesto

DNA represents the student voice at Royal Holloway, harm reduction policies must be implemented on campus instead of punitive action against vulnerable students.

DNA does not encourage or condemn drug and alcohol consumption, we prioritise student’s safety and well being.

We understand that recreational drug and alcohol use is a common part of the university environment.

DNA strives to create a more open conversation about drug use and alcohol consumption.

We believe in a non-stigmatized future to create a safer environment for drug and alcohol users.

DNA campaigns for the support of those who suffer from drug or alcohol dependency and misuse.

We believe that education is the vehicle that will deconstruct taboos, stigma, and misinformation surrounding drug and alcohol use.

DNA believes that students are entitled to education and informion on alcohol and drugs. We believe this will enable students to make safer choices.

Systemic racism fuels the discriminatory measures that underpin drug policies. It is this subtle yet deadly enforcement of racist beliefs that we fight against to finally put an end to the so-called ‘war on drugs’ and to protect the rights of all people affected.
